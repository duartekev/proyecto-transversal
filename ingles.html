Types of Renewable Energy

Solar energy: Solar energy is energy obtained from the sun in the form of electromagnetic radiation (light, heat and ultraviolet rays). By installing solar panels or collectors, it can be used to obtain thermal energy (photothermal system) or to generate electricity (photovoltaic system) and can be of two types;

Photovoltaics, when sunlight is used and transformed into electricity through the use of photovoltaic solar panels, and

Thermal, by taking advantage of the sun's heat through thermal collectors that absorb and concentrate said heat.

Wind energy: Wind energy is a renewable energy source, it does not pollute, is inexhaustible and reduces the use of fossil fuels, the source of greenhouse emissions that cause global warming.It is obtained by transforming the kinetic energy of the wind into electricity. It can be of two types, depending on where the wind turbines are located.

Onshore wind power takes advantage of currents of air produced on land, and

Offshore wind, is nourished by the force of the wind that is produced on the high seas.

Geothermal energy: Geothermal energy is the energy obtained by harnessing the Earth's internal heat, which globally can be considered continuous and inexhaustible on a human scale. A geothermal field is an area of ​​the subsoil where the geothermal resource can be used by man.
